Step 1 · Watch · About 7 minutes

How do we choose the right graph?

Watch the video. Listen for the three bar-chart variations and what histogram, pie, scatter, and line charts help us understand.

Video: “How To Choose The Right Graph (Types of Graphs and When To Use Them)” by UNDATABLE. Open on YouTube.

While you watch: Which bar variation shows ranking? What does a histogram show? Which chart shows change over time?

Step 2 · Explore · About 15 minutes

Explore the five chart families from the video

Study each example. Notice its title, labels, visual shape, and the question it helps answer.

1

Bar chart family

Use them to: compare categories. Turn bars horizontally for ranking, or divide them into stacks to show parts of each total.

They answer: Which category is largest, what is the ranking, or how is each total divided?

2

Histogram

Use it to: show the distribution of numerical data grouped into ranges. Its bars touch because the ranges are continuous.

It answers: Which score range contains the most students?

3

Pie chart

How class time is used

Practice 50%Discussion 30%Reflection 20%

Use it to: show how one whole is divided into a few parts.

It answers: What share of class time is practice?

4

Scatter plot

Use it to: look for a relationship between two numerical variables.

It answers: Do scores tend to rise as study time rises?

5

Line chart

Use it to: show change over time or another meaningful ordered sequence.

It answers: How did temperature change during the week?

Before you continue: Compare the shapes. Category bars have gaps, histogram bars touch, pie slices form a whole, scatter points are separate, and a line connects values in order.

Step 3 · Recognize · About 10 minutes

What type of display is it?

Look at each shape and select its name. Use the examples from the previous step when you need help.

Step 4 · Decide · About 10 minutes

Is this the correct use?

Use the video and examples. Decide whether each chart variation is a good choice for the purpose.

Vertical / column chart We want to compare sales across four product categories. Is this a good choice?

Horizontal bar chart We want to rank ten countries from highest to lowest. Is this a good choice?

Stacked bar chart We want to compare each class total and how much came from two groups. Is this a good choice?

Histogram We want to see how student heights are distributed across numerical ranges. Is this a good choice?

Pie chart We want to show how temperature changes each day for two weeks. Is this a good choice?

Scatter chart We want to see whether study hours and quiz scores are correlated. Is this a good choice?

Line chart We want to show how website visits change each month. Is this a good choice?
